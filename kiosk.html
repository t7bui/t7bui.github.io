Overview

Background

The University of California - San Diego (UCSD) is a beautiful campus that attracts tourists with its many sightseeing attractions. One of the unique attractions of UCSD is the Stuart Collection, a permanent collection of 20 site-specific sculptures commissioned by leading contemporary artists and located throughout the campus. However, starting in 2020, one of the sculptures called Fallen Star was closed to the public, restricting visitors' ability to fully experience its unique intriguing interior.

To address this issue, we propose a plan to design and install a virtual tour kiosk that helps users experience Fallen Star despite its restrictions. In addition to showcasing Fallen Star, the kiosk also provides visitors with short descriptions and locations of the other sculptures in the Stuart Collection, encouraging them to explore the campus and discover the other unique art pieces.

Problem Statement

How might we help UCSD prospective students and visitors experience Fallen Star despite the restrictions and enhance their campus tour experience?

User Research

Fieldwork/Observations

To understand the user environment and identify suitable kiosk locations for installation, we observed the activity around Fallen Star on the top floor of Irwin & Joan Jacobs School of Engineering. After a few days of observation, we noticed that while many people pass by the building, fewer individuals actually venture to the top floor to see the sculpture. As a result, we propose several potential kiosk locations: inside the ground floor of the engineering school, outside the building, outside the Fallen Star door on the top floor, or outside the Irwin & Joan Jacobs School of Engineering building on the ground floor.

Exploratory Interview

In our informal interviews, we gathered insights from 8 individuals, including students and staff members, about their experiences with Fallen Star. We discovered that while some students had limited exposure to the sculpture, there was interest in a virtual tour. The suggestion to install a kiosk on the ground floor of Irwin & Joan Jacobs School of Engineering emerged as a way to enhance accessibility. Additionally, participants emphasized the importance of incorporating a tour guide to provide insights into the artist's intention behind the sculpture.

Persona

After conducting our research, we developed two personas to represent our target audience.

Bobby, a first-year student who has not had a chance to visit Fallen Star, is curious if there is a way for him to explore that sculpture.

Jeff who is a senior, has been inside Fallen Star before but wants to find ways to visit the sculpture again before graduation.

Interview Insights

  • 90% of interviewws suggested Kiosk should locate outside on the ground floor for more accessibility
  • First and second-years, both undergraduates and graduate students, showed higher interest in Fallen Star.
  • Some participants preferred in-person appointments as it offers a more unique experience compared to virtual tours
  • It was apparent that almost all people wanted to understand the meaning behind the art and the artist’s inspiration and intention behind it

Mission Statement

We aim to make Do Ho Suh’s Fallen Star more accessible to the public both conceptually and physically by sharing Suh’s artistic message and background , situating visitors in the interior through the virtual tour, and facilitating in-person appointments with UCSD students & staff through our Fallen Star Kiosk.

What need to improve:

Introduction/Exterior Tour:

  • CONFUSION with White Arrow - Many users thought the White Arrow would lead them inside the house
  • BIG ISSUE with Drag Animation - Many users thought it prompts them to drag the screen (see video below)

Interior Tour:

  • NOT CLEAR with Home Button - users did not know how to exit tour using Home Button (see video below)
  • NOT VISIBLE with Garden Button - users did not realize there is a Garden Button in the corner (see video below)

  • Interesting Observation - when stuck, users try to exit the house by tapping outside the door (see video below)

Stuart Collection Page:

  • Suggestions - Add sculptures' locations and add option to enlarge photos
  • Interesting Observation - everyone's FIRST INSTINCT is to drag the page up
  • BIG ISSUE with Gallery Flow - User 3 notices the gallery flow reset when clicking on

What we changed:

Introduction/Exterior Tour:

  • White Arrow - Have the arrow leads users inside the house (no zoom foward)
  • Drag Animation - Change finger to arrows (might indicate less action)
  • Wording of Instruction - Move action words to the end

Interior Tour:

  • Home Button - Change "Home" under icon to "Exit Tour"
  • Garden Button - Remove this button, and have mascot ask at the door "Want to go outside?"; more natural this way

Stuart Collection Page:

  • Add scrulptures' locations and also have option to see more photos based on suggestions
  • Extend the page a bit longer, so that would allow users to drag up when they enter the page
