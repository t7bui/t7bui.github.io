Fallen Star Kiosk

Goal: To make Fallen Star more acessible despite the restrictions after COVID-19.

Collaborators: Griffen Bengard, Fiona Chen, James Olichney, Caitlin Zakkour

  • Coordinated team and conduct weekly meetings to go over logistics to meet deadlines for project

Overview

Background

The closure of UCSD's Fallen Star sculpture since 2020 has limited access to its captivating interior, despite the campus attracting tourists with its renowned Stuart Collection.

How might we help UCSD prospective students and visitors experience Fallen Star despite the restrictions and enhance their campus tour experience?

Our proposed solution involves the creation and installation of a virtual tour kiosk that allows users to experience Fallen Star, while also providing information on other sculptures in the Stuart Collection, encouraging visitors to explore and discover the campus's unique art pieces.

User Research

Fieldwork/Observation

To understand the user environment and identify suitable kiosk locations for installation, we observed the activity around Fallen Star on the top floor of Irwin & Joan Jacobs School of Engineering. After a few days of observation, we noticed that while many people pass by the building, fewer individuals actually venture to the top floor to see the sculpture.

As a result, we propose several potential kiosk locations:

  • Inside the building on the ground floor
  • Outside infront of the building
  • Outside the Fallen Star door on the top floor

Field Interview

We chatted with 8 UCSD students and staff and discovered:

  • Some students had limited exposure to the sculpture, there was interest in a virtual tour
  • Suggestion to install a kiosk on the ground floor of the building emerged as a way to enhance accessibility
  • Participants emphasized the importance of incorporating a tour guide to provide insights into the artist's intention behind the sculpture

Persona

After conducting our research, we developed two personas to represent our target audience.

Bobby, a first-year student who has not had a chance to visit Fallen Star, is curious if there is a way for him to explore that sculpture.

Jeff who is a senior, has been inside Fallen Star before but wants to find ways to visit the sculpture again before graduation.

Ideation

Storyboards

Interview Insights

  • 90% of interviews suggested Kiosk should locate outside on the ground floor for more accessibility
  • First and second-years, both undergraduates and graduate students, showed higher interest in Fallen Star.
  • Some participants preferred in-person appointments as it offers a more unique experience compared to virtual tours
  • It was apparent that almost all people wanted to understand the meaning behind the art and the artist’s inspiration and intention behind it

We strive to increase accessibility for the public to Do Ho Suh's Fallen Star by sharing his artistic message and background, providing a virtual tour with tour guide, and arranging in-person appointments for UCSD students and staff.

Prototype

Low Fidelity

With a good understanding of Fallen Star Kiosk's features, we each individually create low fidelity wireframes of how each of us see Fallen Star.



Feedbacks and Suggestions for Low-Fidelity:

  1. Lack of indication to drag: Participants were unaware they could drag in virtual tour page, resulting in many users not exploring it.
  2. Tap on door to enter: Users expected to enter the house by tapping on the door, but this feature was not ready in low-fi.
  3. Change homepage to Fallen Star theme: Participants suggested aligning the homepage with Fallen Star theme for more cohesive and visually appealing experience
  4. Limited appointments access to UCSD students & staff: Users recommended making appointments exclusive to UCSD students and staff instead of being open to public
  5. Improve info button functionality: Users suggested we should allow users to tap anywhere on screen to exit information overlay, rather than tapping back on the (i) icon

Usability Testing

What need to improve:

Introduction/Exterior Tour:

  • CONFUSION with White Arrow - Many users thought the White Arrow would lead them inside the house
  • To fix this: we have the arrow leads users straight into Interior Tour
  • BIG ISSUE with Drag Animation - Many users thought it prompts them to drag the screen
  • To fix this: we change the animation of drag with arrow, and move the action word to the end

Interior Tour:

  • NOT CLEAR with Home Button - users did not know how to exit tour using Home Button
  • To fix this: we change "Home" to "Exit Tour"
  • Interesting Observation - when stuck, users try to exit the house by tapping outside the door
  • To fix this: we have our tour guide ask if users finish touring interior or not

Stuart Collection Page:

  • Suggestions - Add sculptures' locations and add option to enlarge photos
  • BIG ISSUE with Gallery Flow - User 3 notices the gallery flow reset when clicking on

Reflections

What I learned

  • First-time team management experience taught me valuable lessons in effective communication, planning, and task delegation.
  • Active involvement in interview processes, UI design, and usability testing expanded my skills in interviewing, receiving feedback, and utilizing Figma for design enhancements.

If we have more time

  • Unresolved issue: Usability test revealed confusion with the dragging animation for exterior tour. Given more time, we would enhance the interaction for better user intuitiveness.
  • Consistency suggestion: Consider incorporating virtual tour format for Stuart Collection Gallery to align with Fallen Star and meet Prof's feedback.
  • Enhanced usability: Enable dragging while Yellow Button is clicked to prevent information text cutoff confusion during exterior tour.
  • Text summarization opportunity: Given additional time, we could have summarized the Stuart Collection Gallery texts in our own words instead of using direct website excerpts.
  • Navigation optimization: More testing needed to determine the most effective method for guiding users' attention to the arrow navigation in the interior tour.
  • Limited kiosk testing: Due to time constraints, the physical kiosk iterations were based on member feedback rather than user testing.
