Overview

Background

The University of California - San Diego (UCSD) is a beautiful campus that attracts tourists with its many sightseeing attractions. One of the unique attractions of UCSD is the Stuart Collection, a permanent collection of 20 site-specific sculptures commissioned by leading contemporary artists and located throughout the campus. However, starting in 2020, one of the sculptures called Fallen Star was closed to the public, restricting visitors' ability to fully experience its unique intriguing interior.

To address this issue, we propose a plan to design and install a virtual tour kiosk that helps users experience Fallen Star despite its restrictions. In addition to showcasing Fallen Star, the kiosk also provides visitors with short descriptions and locations of the other sculptures in the Stuart Collection, encouraging them to explore the campus and discover the other unique art pieces.

Problem Statement

How might we help UCSD prospective students and visitors experience Fallen Star despite the restrictions and enhance their campus tour experience?

User Research

Fieldwork/Observations

The goal of this step is to understand the user environment and think of possible kiosk locations for installation. Since Fallen Star is on the top floor of Irwin & Joan Jacobs School of Engineering, is it busy up there during the week? Where could our kiosk be located so that it is accessible to everyone?
After 2-3 days of observations, we found that people are many people walk around in front of Irwin & Joan Jacobs School of Engineering, but not that many people go all the way to the top floor to see Fallen Star. 
We came up with some possible locations for the kiosk. One is to have it on the ground floor inside of Irwin & Joan Jacobs School of Engineering, two is to have one outside of the building, three is to have one on the top floor outside of Fallen Star door, and one on the ground floor outside of Irwin & Joan Jacobs School of Engineering building.

Exploratory Interview

During our informal interviews we want to gain more insights on what are the experiences that users have with Fallen Star thus far and also ask for suggestions of possible kiosk locations. We interviewed around 8 people, ranging from first-year students to staff members to gain more insights about the experiences they have had with Fallen Star thus far. We also ask about what they want to see on a virtual tour if we have one for Fallen Star and the possible location for it.
We found that while many first and second-year students have not had the opportunity to see Fallen Star, some were able to visit for classroom purposes. Many people suggested that it would be more accessible for everyone if we install the kiosk outside on the ground floor of Irwin & Joan Jacobs School of Engineering. Furthermore, we should incorporate a tour guide to tell the story behind the artist’s intention for this sculpture.

Persona

After conducting our research, we developed two personas to represent our target audience.

Bobby, a first-year student who has not had a chance to visit Fallen Star, is curious if there is a way for him to explore that sculpture.

Jeff who is a senior, has been inside Fallen Star before but wants to find ways to visit the sculpture again before graduation.

Scenarios/Storyboard Sketches

Problem with Appointment:
Jeff is taking a walk around the UCSD campus. He has been around a while now, so he knows that Fallen Star exists but doesn’t know much about it and isn’t interested enough to visit it. While passing by WLH, he notices a new kiosk in front of the engineering building, right under Fallen Star. Curious, he explores the kiosk and is enamored by the deeper meaning and the engineering background behind the art installation. He becomes interested in Fallen Star and makes plans to visit it in person in the future.

Kiosk Location Problem
Bobby is a first-year student at UCSD wandering around to explore the new campus during the weekend. He saw an interesting tilted house on top of the Engineering building near Warren. He wants to get into the building, but it is locked. While looking around, he discovered a Kiosk about that tilted house and found out the house's actual name is Fallen Star. After looking through all the information and a short virtual tour of the building, he is glad that there is something to look at while he could not see the house up close and get the in-person experience.
